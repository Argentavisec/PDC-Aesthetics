Are There Risks with Lip Fillers?

Like any medical treatment, lip fillers do carry some risks, but when performed correctly they are minimal. Common side effects include swelling, redness, or slight bruising, which usually subside within a few days.

Rare Risks Include:

  • Allergic reaction (extremely rare)
  • Uneven swelling (usually resolves naturally)
  • Vascular occlusion (very rare but treatable if recognised early)

The best way to reduce these risks is to choose a medically qualified practitioner like PDC Aesthetics, rather than non-medical providers.

Are Lip Fillers Safer Than Other Treatments?

Clients often compare fillers with treatments like Botox or skin boosters. While each treatment is safe when performed by a qualified professional, fillers are particularly effective for reshaping and volumising lips.

Fillers vs Botox

Botox relaxes muscles to reduce wrinkles but cannot add volume. Fillers are the only option for plumper, more hydrated lips.
