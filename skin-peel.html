AHA vs BHA vs Light TCA — Which Suits You?

AHA Peels (e.g., Glycolic / Lactic)

Brighten, hydrate and smooth

  • Targets dullness, uneven tone and fine texture
  • Suitable for normal–dry or dehydrated skin
  • Minimal downtime with mobile-appropriate strengths

BHA Peel (Salicylic)

Decongest & refine pores

  • Oil-soluble—great for congestion and breakouts
  • Helps appearance of enlarged pores and blackheads
  • Often paired with gentle AHAs for glow + clarity

Light TCA Peel

Tone & texture boost

  • Improves superficial texture and uneven tone
  • Used conservatively for safe home-visit protocols
  • Expect a few days of light flaking with aftercare

We’ll assess your skin, routine and timeline to choose the right active blend and strength.

What Peels Can Help With

  • Dullness and uneven tone
  • Congestion and visible pores
  • Early fine lines and texture
  • Non-inflammatory pigmentation (sun/age-related)
  • Pre-event smoothness and makeup prep

For acne scarring or deeper texture change, consider adding microneedling for collagen support.

Aftercare (Chemical Peels at Home)

  • First 24–48 hours: avoid heat, vigorous exercise, exfoliants and retinoids
  • Expect mild redness and light flaking for 1–3 days depending on peel type
  • Use gentle cleanser, bland moisturiser and daily broad-spectrum SPF
  • No picking or scratching; allow flaking to shed naturally
  • We’ll check in and adjust the plan based on your response
